编织我的梦
Weaving my dreams
By Linda Liukas
The dream of this TED speaker, Ms. Linda Liukas, is to build her dream world. She is looking forward to waking up in the morning in Muming Valley, strolling on the planet of Tatoo in the afternoon, and dreaming in Narnia at night. The way to help her achieve this dream is computer programming, which many people think are obscure, boring, and men-led disciplines.
爱情应有的样子
What love should look like
By Mandy Len Catron
When talking about love, the words that people use most often have “falling in love”, “spirited”, “tea does not think about rice”, “loves are disasters”, “love is difficult to control”, “pain and happiness”, etc. Have we ever wondered if these words have given us some negative psychological cues, which have caused many people to misunderstand and misinterpret the feelings and meanings of love? Can we have better words or metaphors to talk about love?
听故事是不够的
Listening to the story is not enough
By Sisonke Msimang
Good stories are a must, but they are not as magical as we think. On the contrary, good stories tend to paralyze people's minds and create an illusion, as if the world is better because they are real, but real. The situation is not what we imagined. So what is the truth? What can really help us make the world a better place?
算法如何塑造我们的世界
How does the algorithm shape our world?
By Kevin Slavin
Kevin Slavin believes that we live in a world designed for algorithms — and increasingly controlled by algorithms. In this wonderful speech from TEDGlobal, he showed how these complex computer programs are determined: espionage strategies, stock prices, movie scripts and architecture. He warned that we are writing code that we can't understand and that may be out of control.
改变命运的那个机会
The opportunity to change the destiny
By Christopher Ategeka
Talent can be everyone, but the opportunity is not. From the homeless orphan to the world's top prestigious double degree, what changed his destiny? Why does he have such an opportunity? How can more people have such opportunities? True love can transcend blood, transcend territory, transcend culture, everyone is important, and everyone's dreams are important.
手语传情 让爱发声
Sign language, let love sound
By Christine Sun Kim
The speaker, Ms. Christine Sun Kim is a monk and a paint artist. She compares her hand to music, because it is colorful, beautiful and moving, and can also be used to convey feelings. Sign language is a valuable social asset. It is not only deaf-mute people who can learn sign language, just as it is not only those with sound hearings who can learn music.
